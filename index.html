Welcom!

Indeed, my full name is Abdolhamid Dashtiahangar! So, you'll find different flavors of this 23 charachters name in my publications:). I usually go with Hamid Dashti, tho. I recently recieved my PhD frm Boise State University in Geosciences. Currently, I'm a postdoc at the University of Arizona working on carbon and energy cycle in the Arctic and boreal regions. My MSc is in GIS and remote sensing from Shahid Beheshti University, Iran. My primary interest is remote sensing (optical and lidar) and dynamic global vegetation modeling of terrestrial ecossytems. These days my focus is on data science to answer questions related to energy and carbon cycles using large geospatial (mostly remote sensing products) datasets and CLM model as part of NASA Arctic-Boreal Vulnerability Experiment (NASA ABoVE).

During my PhD I was working on charachterizing drylands vegetation. By incorporating the concept of p-theory (basically averaging radiative transfer in vegetation) I showed the uncertainity of machine learning approaches in estimating foliar nitrogen from hyperspectral data. I also calibrated the Ecosystem Demography (ED) DGVM in drylands. For the first time, We developed a shrub palnt functional type for this model. Also, I am interested in lidar and optical data fusion. I recently developed a framework to mechanistically link these sensors.
